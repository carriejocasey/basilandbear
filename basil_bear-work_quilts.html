My mom is a big quilter and she bought me my first sewing machine for Christmas in 2013. Since then, I've sewn a variety of smallish projects, but my biggest sewing accomplishments have been my first three quilts! What I enjoy about making quilts is the patterns, the colors, the laying out and arranging that must be done. What I don't enjoy...is the actual sewing! Ok, ok not really, that part isn't that bad! I find I enjoy myself when I'm not following a super defined pattern. It's fun to experiment and see what happens! I'm looking forward to time put towards more tactile projects like these.

the first one

My first quilt made with my first sewing machine! I chose the fabrics for their cheerfulness. I thought they would brighten up the cold winter days as I snuggled under my new blanket. I have a lot more fun sewing when I'm not tied to a pattern. the back of this quilt was a fun experiment and contrast to the structured pattern of the front.

happy baby

This quilt was made for my friends, the Atchos and their newest family addition, Adrian! Sewing the chevrons was made surprisingly "easy" by pinning marked papers to the fabric and literally sewing on the dotted line. My mom's friend, Wendy, did a beautiful job quilting this with the cutest dinosaur pattern!

celebrate in color

Made for my wonderful friends, Corey and Elizabeth, in celebration of their marriage! This quilt was a lot of fun to put together. The pattern is called a "double-slice layer cake" and leaves a lot of the layout up to chance! I chose to arrange the blocks from lightest to darkest/by the colors of the rainbow diagonally. Wendy did yet another amazing job of quilting with a fun bubbly pattern.
